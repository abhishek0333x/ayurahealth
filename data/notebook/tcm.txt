# Traditional Chinese Medicine (NotebookLM Source)

## Core Principles
1. **Yin and Yang**: The complementary and opposing forces (Cold/Heat, Moving/Still, Inside/Outside, Empty/Full).
2. **Qi (Life Force Energy)**: Circulates through the body in meridians (channels).
3. **The Five Elements (Wu Xing)**:
   - Wood (Liver/Gallbladder)
   - Fire (Heart/Small Intestine)
   - Earth (Spleen/Stomach)
   - Metal (Lung/Large Intestine)
   - Water (Kidney/Bladder)
4. **Zang-Fu Organs**: The functional relationships between the internal organs and their energetic pathways.

## Diagnostic Methodologies
- **Tongue Diagnosis**: Color, shape, coating, and moisture.
- **Pulse Palette**: 28 different pulse qualities used to determine organ health.
- **Pattern Differentiation (Zheng)**: Identifying the underlying syndrome rather than the disease label.

## Treatments
- **Acupuncture**: Regulating Qi flow via fine needles at meridian points.
- **Moxibustion**: Warming meridians with burning mugwort (Moxa).
- **Herbal Formulas**: Customized decoctions based on the specific pattern (e.g., Liu Wei Di Huang Wan).
- **Tui Na**: Therapeutic massage and bone manipulation.
- **Qi Gong/Tai Chi**: Movement and breath to cultivate internal energy.
